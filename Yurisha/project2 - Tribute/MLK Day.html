MLK Day



Martin Luther King Jr. Day is a federal holiday in the United States. It marks the birthday of Martin Luther King Jr. It is observed on the third Monday of January each year.
Martin Luther King Jr. was the chief spokesperson for nonviolent activism in the Civil Rights Movement, which protested racial discrimination in federal and state law. The campaign for a federal holiday in King's honor began soon after his assassination in 1968.

Martin Luther King wanted equal rights for every American citizen. He fought against the racial discrimination in USA. He is truly an inspiration for all of us to fight against discrimination and violence of rights. His words provides us the faith that if we want to eradicate any social evil, we can by our efforts. Martin Luther King Day honors the life of a Martin Luther King who fought for racial justice and equality. It celebrates equality in a society that he hoped to become color-blind. He is forever respected and honoured for his deeds and advices. To remember his acts, MLK Day is celebrated. It was officially observed in all 50 states for the first time in 2000.
